## Stale brief suppression in recall

Briefs were going stale and cluttering every `/recall`: `recall()` surfaced the active brief unconditionally (gated only on `status === 'active'`), with no freshness signal or lifecycle pressure.

**What:** Recall now suppresses a stale active brief and emits a one-line action-oriented nudge instead. Non-destructive — brief status on disk is never mutated.

**How:**
- `findLatestCheckpointTimestampForBrief()` in `checkpoints.ts` — newest-first scan, early-exits at first matching date dir, matches `briefId` + legacy `planId`.
- `resolveActiveBrief()` in `recall.ts` with `STALE_BRIEF_DAYS = 7`. Staleness signal = newest referencing checkpoint, falling back to `brief.created`. Stale when `floor((now - lastActivity)/day) > 7`.
- `RecallResult.staleBrief` (`StaleBriefNotice`) in `types.ts`.
- Handler renders `⚠️ Active brief "X" untouched Nd — complete or archive it...`; header shows `+ stale brief notice`.

**Scope:** recall-only (per design). Cross-workspace `'all'` path untouched — it never surfaced a brief. `/handoff`, `/standup`, `/brief-status` deliberately out of scope.
